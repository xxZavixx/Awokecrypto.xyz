What is Cryptocurrency?

Introduction to Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security. It operates on decentralized networks based on blockchain technology.

Unlike traditional currencies issued by central banks, cryptocurrencies are decentralized and allow peer-to-peer transactions without intermediaries.

Blockchain Technology

At the heart of cryptocurrency is blockchain technology. It is a distributed ledger that ensures secure, transparent, and tamper-proof records of transactions.

Blockchain enables trustless systems, meaning that transactions can be verified without relying on a trusted third party, making it revolutionary for various industries.

Uses and Applications

Cryptocurrencies are used for various applications, including online payments, investments, and supporting decentralized applications (dApps).

They are also enabling new financial ecosystems, such as DeFi (Decentralized Finance), which provides financial services without traditional banks.
